# NOOP v8.6.1

A polish + fixes release on top of 8.6.0: a strap restart button, real battery savings on Android, Health Connect finally usable on Android 13, and a handful of fixes. Everything runs on your own device, offline, no account. Update from the Releases page, AltStore, or `brew upgrade`.

## New

- **Restart strap (#166).** The connected band's actions menu in Devices gains a **Restart strap** option — a confirmation-gated, non-destructive reboot (your stored data is kept). Useful when a strap is stuck or misbehaving, without switching to the official app. The card shows a **Reconnecting…** state while the strap boots and returns to **Active · Live** once it's back. iPhone, Mac and Android; verified on WHOOP 4.0 and 5.0.

## Fixed

- **Lighter on battery (Android) (#228).** Two drains fixed: NOOP re-ran the strap's history offload every 15 minutes for the whole connection even when nothing new was banked (waking the strap's storage on a fixed cadence), and on a band whose Bluetooth was contended by the official WHOOP app it hammered an active reconnect indefinitely. It now backs off the empty offloads and steps down to a low-power reconnect when the link keeps flapping. Thanks tanarchytan.
- **Health Connect on Android 13 (#226).** NOOP was missing from Health Connect's **App permissions** list on Android 13 (a wrong permission-rationale declaration), so there was no way to grant access and import data. It now appears and is grantable. Android 14+ was unaffected.
- **Auto-detected workouts save (Android) (#214).** Tapping **Save** on a "looks like a workout" suggestion silently failed — the save was cancelled the instant the card dismissed itself — so it never appeared in your data and the card kept re-prompting. It now saves reliably and stops re-asking.
- **Oura ring wear state (#218).** With an Oura ring streaming live heart rate, the Live screen read **Wear State: Unknown**; it now reads **On wrist** while the ring is streaming (experimental Oura path; iPhone and Mac).
- **Re-tapping the active tab returns you to the top (#135).** On iPhone and iPad, tapping the tab you're already on now pops back to that tab's main page from a subpage — and scrolls to the top when you're already at the root — with a smooth animation instead of a hard rebuild.

## Notes

- Under-the-hood groundwork with no user-facing change: WHOOP 5 `type-0x2F` optical-region decoding and a 16th deep-data flag from a real capture (#103), plus alarm-readback and strap-reboot diagnostics for the strap log. These help future work and triage; they don't alter your scores.
