# NOOP v8.7.0

A visibility + polish release on top of 8.6.2: a compact sync indicator on Today for everyone, an honest warning when a strap's clock is set wrong, a more reliable WHOOP 4.0 wake alarm, a menu tidy-up, and the missing German translations filled in. Everything still runs on your own device, offline, no account. Update from the Releases page, AltStore, or `brew upgrade`.

## New

- **A sync indicator on Today, for everyone (#245).** Sync feedback used to live only on the Live screen, and the "Syncing strap history…" note only appeared for users still building their first scores — so an established user, and especially a WHOOP 5.0 / MG owner whose history offloads are rare, saw nothing on Today. There's now a compact chip in the Today header with three states: a spinner and a live chunk count while history is downloading, a "synced N ago" once it's caught up, and a "live" marker for a 5/MG whose history sync is still experimental. It shows nothing only on a genuine cold start. iPhone, Mac and Android.

## Fixed

- **A strap with a future-dated clock imported nothing, silently (#324).** If a strap's internal clock relatches to a date far in the future (its banked history reads years ahead), NOOP correctly refuses to import that data — filing it under the wrong dates would scramble your dashboard. But it did so quietly, so it looked like a clean sync that brought back nothing. NOOP now tells you plainly that the strap's clock is set in the future and what to do about it: fully charge the strap to 100%, then power-cycle it so its clock re-syncs. iPhone, Mac and Android.
- **The WHOOP 4.0 wake alarm could be set but not fire (#34).** The smart wake alarm re-arms itself on the strap every time it reconnects, but that re-arm was firing in the middle of the connect handshake — before the strap's reply channel was confirmed ready — so the alarm's read-back was dropped and the alarm time didn't reliably land. The re-arm now waits until the connection has fully settled (handshake done and the command-response channel confirmed live), so the alarm time reaches the strap. Thanks digitalerdude.
- **Duplicate settings and colliding labels (#336).** Several controls appeared in two places on the same storage — the same toggle rendered under both Settings and the Test Centre, a backup action duplicated across screens, an "Only when worn" gate written from two menus — which was confusing and easy to get out of sync. The duplicates are removed (each control now has one home), the two identically-named "Broadcast heart rate" toggles are renamed so you can tell them apart (strap broadcast for Garmin/ANT gear vs. broadcasting live HR from your phone), a couple of dead placeholder toggles are gone, and developer-only diagnostics moved into the Test Centre. No feature lost its home. Thanks tanarchytan.

## Localization

- **German filled in across the app (#326).** The reported missing German strings turned out to be the tip of a much larger gap: the shared chart and component package used on nearly every screen — the sleep hypnogram, trend charts, the recovery calendar, the HR chart — was almost entirely untranslated, along with the Apple Watch app and its complications, which is why untranslated fragments kept surfacing across unrelated screens. All of it is filled in now, matching the terminology already used elsewhere, plus a fix so package-level strings actually resolve against their own translations. Thanks digitalerdude.

## Notes

- Under-the-hood, no user-facing change: when a strap's clock is set in the future, the strap log now captures the RTC-reset events and the exact date range of the refused records, so the cause is diagnosable from a shared log rather than a silent drop (#324). A WHOOP 4.0 sleep-onset diagnostic line was added to measure how early bedtime detection opens on a sparse-motion night, ahead of tuning it (#271). And on the experimental Oura path, the green-LED IBI tag was gated out of live scoring because it was being decoded with the wrong layout and could corrupt reconstructed HRV — pending a verified decoder (#333). These aid triage and future work; they don't change your scores.
