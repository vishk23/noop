# NOOP v9.0.0

A major release on top of 8.7.0. The headline is **Power saving** — battery-adaptive behaviour built around *your strap's* charge, not your phone's — alongside a Gemini-powered AI Coach on Android, a big expansion of metric detail and trend windows, a wave of sleep and recovery accuracy fixes, and the first real decoding of the WHOOP 5.0/MG motion sensors. Everything still runs on your own device, offline, no account. Update from the Releases page, AltStore, or `brew upgrade`.

## New

- **Power saving (#477).** A new Settings → Power saving section, keyed on the connected strap's battery (WHOOP, and the same for Oura/Fitbit). When your strap is running low and discharging, NOOP eases the load on it: it stretches its background history sync from every 15 minutes to every 45, and — as a sub-option — releases the always-on continuous-HRV stream, the biggest continuous drain on the band. You choose the strap-battery level it starts at (10–30%); it's off by default, never runs while the strap is charging, and a Live screen still shows heart rate on demand. Nothing is lost — the strap banks its own data, so a stretched sync just arrives in larger, less frequent batches. iPhone, Mac and Android.
- **Google Gemini in the AI Coach on Android (#400).** Android now has the native Gemini provider that iPhone and Mac already carried, so the coach and your model choice behave identically across platforms. On-device, opt-in, and it only talks to Google if you enable it and supply your own key.
- **Key Metrics detail + selectable trend windows (#430, #432, #433, #435).** Key Metrics gains a Detailed-tiles option with tap-through trend detail, and every metric's detail timeline now offers 1D / 2D / 2W / … / 3M / 6M / 1Y / All windows so you can zoom your history to the range you want — matched on iPhone, Mac and Android.
- **Arrange your Today screen (#428).** iPhone and Mac gain a persisted section order with an Arrange sheet, matching Android.
- **Recovery Index + Activity Balance in Charge (#417).** Two new Charge drivers land on both platforms, wired in behind the scenes ahead of surfacing them.
- **Coarse workout-type hint (#414).** An opt-in, advisory classifier that suggests a broad activity type for a detected workout — it never overrides what you set. Both platforms.
- **Keep NOOP alive overnight, on Android (#386).** An opt-in toggle that walks you through the battery-optimisation exemption some phones need so an overnight re-score isn't killed in the background.

## Fixed

- **A carried Charge could show the wrong source (#412).** When Today carried last night's Charge before tonight scored, the source badge could read "Whoop" for a value NOOP computed on-device; it now names the real source. Thanks kavemang.
- **The "Calibrating N of 4" count could stall after a recalibrate (#393).** After a manual HRV-baseline recalibration, the count folded in pre-recalibration nights and could tip past the seed gate, so the score side wrongly read "Was your strap worn?" instead of "Calibrating." It now tracks the baseline's real post-recalibration count.
- **Elevated HR on a still wrist scored as awake (#462).** Sleep staging now requires motion to corroborate a wake call, so a hot-but-motionless stretch is no longer misread as an awakening.
- **Split nights under-reported sleep (#345).** The headline Asleep total and the hypnogram axis now span the whole bridged night, not just the main session.
- **A sleep-staging tune over-called "awake" for healthy sleepers (#431).** The pre-#348 staging defaults are restored after the newer wake tune read too aggressively on real nights.
- **Today could sit blank after a killed overnight re-score (#386).** NOOP now catches up the score the moment you open it, on Android.
- **Live-BLE installs exported an empty CSV (#458).** The export now reads the active strap's data union, so a device that has never imported a WHOOP file still exports its banked history.
- **A stuck Bluetooth-permission state (#391, #429).** NOOP now tells a transient permission hiccup apart from a genuinely wedged one — no false "re-grant" banner, and no unbounded silence.

## WHOOP 5.0 / MG

- **6-axis motion decoded, for research (#423).** The 5.0/MG banks a 100 Hz accelerometer + gyroscope buffer during its offload; NOOP now decodes it and extracts activity features (cadence, energy, jerk), and can passively capture the strap's high-rate sensor buffers to a local file when you enable capture — the foundation for real activity/sport detection on the 5.0/MG. Decode is byte-identical across iPhone, Mac and Android. Thanks vishk23 and tanarchytan.
- **Raw PPG waveform stored (#156).** The v26 record's raw optical waveform is now persisted, groundwork for the SpO₂ / blood-pressure work still being figured out.

## Localization

- **A translation safety net (#452).** A new check flags any new screen text that ships without its German translation, or a control that was never wired for translation at all, so gaps get caught before release rather than after a report. Thanks digitalerdude.
- **German across Trends, Explore and the Deep Timeline (#451).** These three Android screens are now fully translated. Thanks digitalerdude.
- **Russian filled in (#457).** Missing Russian strings are completed. Thanks digitalerdude.

## Notes

- Under the hood, no user-facing change: NOOP now tracks a per-device baseline "era" so switching straps (which can shift the HRV scale) doesn't quietly corrupt your recovery baseline (#459), and a set of sleep and capture diagnostics were added to the strap log to aid triage. These don't change your scores.
