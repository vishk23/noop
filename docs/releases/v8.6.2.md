# NOOP v8.6.2

A features + fixes release on top of 8.6.1: your data can now flow into Apple Health, imported rides feed your day's Effort, a batch of sleep-detection and sleep-display fixes recover nights that were being dropped or mis-timed, and several automatic-sync stalls are fixed. Everything still runs on your own device, offline, no account. Update from the Releases page, AltStore, or `brew upgrade`.

## New

- **Write your data to Apple Health (iPhone) (#249).** NOOP now writes sleep stages, minute-by-minute heart rate, and your workouts back to Apple Health, so the health data NOOP computes on-device is available to the rest of your phone. Opt-in through the normal Health permissions prompt. Thanks vishk23.
- **Imported rides light your day's Effort (#137).** Previously an imported GPX / TCX / FIT workout was stored but its heart rate never counted toward anything. Now, on a day the strap wasn't worn, the ride's real per-sample heart rate becomes that day's heart-rate source and lights the Effort ring — computed from the measured data, exactly as for a worn strap, with no fabricated strain. When the strap *was* worn, or a whole-day WHOOP import covers the day, those win — the ride only ever fills a genuinely empty day. iPhone, Mac and Android.
- **Predictive low-battery alert (#250).** NOOP now warns you when your strap is down to roughly a day of estimated runtime, so a dead strap doesn't catch you out overnight. iPhone, Mac and Android. Thanks vishk23.
- **Refresh on open (#267).** Bringing NOOP to the foreground now nudges a fresh sync instead of waiting on the background timer — throttled so it can't fire more than once every 90 seconds and costs nothing on a warm link. iPhone, Mac and Android. Thanks digitalerdude.

## Fixed

- **Sleep nights dropped as "no sleep recorded" (#268).** A night with a handful of brief arousal or wake heart-rate spikes (up to ~190 bpm) could pull the average over the sleep threshold and get the whole night rejected — so a normal night read as no sleep at all. Detection now uses the spike-robust median instead of the mean, recovering exactly those nights while still rejecting genuinely awake stretches. Validated against public sleep datasets with gold-standard PSG labels. iPhone, Mac and Android. Thanks tanarchytan.
- **Wrong bedtime and impossible sleep totals after an edit (#259).** On some nights the Sleep tab showed a bedtime hours too early (e.g. 1 am for a 6 am sleep) while the edit picker showed the right time, and the stage total could read as *more* asleep than time in bed. A stray early sleep fragment was hijacking the displayed bedtime, and a hand-edited bedtime wasn't trimming the stored stages. Both are fixed: the displayed bedtime, hypnogram, stage totals and score now all agree on the corrected time, and sleep can never exceed time in bed. iPhone, Mac and Android. Thanks tanarchytan.
- **Sleep source precedence (#241).** When both a WHOOP import and NOOP's own computed night exist for a day, the richer source (the one carrying real stages) is chosen consistently, and an imported night no longer loses to a stage-less computed sibling. iPhone, Mac and Android. Thanks TheBoroer.
- **Automatic sync could stall until you reconnected (#266).** On Android, a strap whose clock briefly read ahead of real time latched a "clock untrusted" state that then blocked every automatic sync — freezing the WHOOP 4.0 battery reading and stopping history offload — until a full reconnect. The check is now re-evaluated on every sync attempt, so it clears itself once the clock catches up. Thanks digitalerdude.
- **A rare first-sync stall on iPhone (#261).** On a cold background relaunch right after updating, two parts of the app could open the local database at the same moment and collide, deferring that sync. Store opening is now serialized so it can't happen.
- **"Connected · not paired" for a WHOOP 5.0 / MG that refused to bond (#221).** A 5.0 / MG strap that connects but rejects bonding now says so plainly in Devices instead of showing a misleading "Active · Live". Thanks digitalerdude.
- **Android double-counted steps and calories in Health Connect (#249).** NOOP was writing strap steps and calories to Health Connect where another source already had them; it now leaves those to the phone, matching the iPhone behaviour.
- **Wind-down reminder permission on iPhone (#251).** The wind-down nudge now requests notification permission properly and tells you if it was denied, instead of silently doing nothing. Thanks tigercraft4.
- **Portuguese (Portugal) translation cleaned up (#248).** A number of pt-PT strings had been contaminated with Spanish; they're corrected. Thanks tigercraft4.
- **Home sleep tile now shows its source (#110).** The Today sleep tile labels itself **Whoop · last night** or **On-device · last night**, matching the Sleep tab, so an imported figure is never silently shown as last night's on-device sleep. Thanks digitalerdude.
- **Oura ring: cleaner framing and an honest activity estimate (#234).** The experimental Oura path reads its packets more robustly, improves the sleep-phase read, and stops over-claiming activity. Thanks pipiche38.

## Notes

- Under-the-hood, no user-facing change: the always-on HRV diagnostic in the strap log now records heartbeat-interval coverage and duplicate-beat counts (#257) to pin down an over-count some nights showed, and a gated WHOOP 4.0 reboot probe was added to help find the real reboot frame (#235). These aid triage and future work; they don't change your scores.
